## Short description

Your private, local-first music server and player.

## Description

Parson turns your own music library into a fast, personal streaming experience.
Choose the folders you already keep on your computer, let Parson build a local
catalog, and listen through the desktop app or a web browser on your trusted
network.

Browse albums and artists, search your collection, view lyrics, create
playlists, like tracks, and keep listening history without sending your library
to a Parson cloud service. Parson is self-hosted and open source: your database,
settings, accounts, and music remain under your control.

Parson includes the local server and desktop player in one installer. No
subscription or Parson account is required.

## Features

- Play your own local music collection
- Browse albums, artists, genres, and songs
- Search your library quickly
- Lyrics, playlists, likes, queues, and listening history
- Listen from other devices on your trusted local network
- Self-hosted with no Parson cloud account
- No usage analytics or automatic crash reporting
- Open source under GPL-3.0-only

## Review notes

Parson starts a bundled local server on TCP port 1993 and opens its user
interface in the desktop window. On first launch, follow setup and select a
folder containing non-DRM audio files. No external account, purchase, or test
credentials are required. Windows Firewall may ask whether to allow access for
other devices; local desktop testing works without granting public-network
access.

The application does not include music. The reviewer supplies their own test
audio. Supported formats are documented in the project documentation.
